How to Play STEAM Quiz
Game Rules:
  1. Think of any STEAM personality from the available characters list
  2. Answer the questions with Yes or No
  3. The game will try to guess your character in the fewest steps possible
  4. You can see your progress in the Steps counter
  5. Use the Characters button to see all available personalities
Available Characters
Scientists:
  • Albert Einstein (1879-1955)

    Revolutionary physicist known for the theory of relativity. Nobel Prize winner in Physics (1921). Changed our understanding of space, time, and gravity.

  • Niels Bohr (1885-1962)

    Pioneer in quantum mechanics and atomic structure. Nobel Prize in Physics (1922). Developed the model of the atom with electron orbits.

  • Richard Feynman (1918-1988)

    Quantum physics pioneer and inspiring educator. Nobel Prize in Physics (1965). Known for his work on quantum electrodynamics.

  • Isaac Newton (1643-1727)

    Discovered gravity and laws of motion. Key figure in the scientific revolution. His work laid the foundation for classical mechanics.

  • Charles Darwin (1809-1882)

    Developed the theory of evolution by natural selection. His book "On the Origin of Species" is a cornerstone of modern biology.

  • Marie Curie (1867-1934)

    Discovered radioactivity, first woman to win Nobel Prize. Her research was crucial to the development of X-rays in surgery.

  • Louis Pasteur (1822-1895)

    Father of microbiology, developed pasteurization. His work on vaccines, including rabies, saved countless lives.

  • Alan Turing (1912-1954)

    Father of computer science and artificial intelligence. Key figure in breaking the Enigma code during WWII.

  • John von Neumann (1903-1957)

    Computer architecture pioneer and mathematical genius. Made significant contributions to game theory and quantum mechanics.

  • Rachel Carson (1907-1964)

    Pioneer of environmental science and conservation. Her book "Silent Spring" challenged pesticide use and inspired environmental movements.

  • George Washington Carver (1864-1943)

    Revolutionary agricultural scientist and inventor. Developed crop rotation and alternative crops to cotton, improving soil depleted by cotton monoculture.

Artists:
  • Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519)

    Renaissance polymath, painter of Mona Lisa. His works encompass painting, sculpture, architecture, and science.

  • Michelangelo (1475-1564)

    Sculptor and painter of the Sistine Chapel. His sculptures, like David, are masterpieces of Renaissance art.

  • Vincent van Gogh (1853-1890)

    Post-impressionist painter of Starry Night. His expressive use of color and brushwork had a profound influence on 20th-century art.

  • Pablo Picasso (1881-1973)

    Pioneer of Cubism and modern art. His diverse body of work includes painting, sculpture, and ceramics.

Mathematicians & Computer Scientists:
  • Ada Lovelace (1815-1852)

    First computer programmer in history. Her notes on the Analytical Engine include what is considered the first algorithm.

  • Grace Hopper (1906-1992)

    Invented first compiler and programming pioneer. Her work led to the development of COBOL, one of the first high-level programming languages.

  • Pythagoras (c. 570-495 BC)

    Ancient Greek mathematician, discovered Pythagorean theorem. His work in mathematics and philosophy laid the groundwork for future scholars.

  • Katherine Johnson (1918-2020)

    NASA mathematician who calculated space flight trajectories. Her calculations were critical to the success of the early space missions.

  • David Hilbert (1862-1943)

    Influential mathematician who formalized mathematical logic. His work laid the foundation for much of modern mathematics.

Inventors & Architects:
  • Thomas Edison (1847-1931)

    Inventor of the light bulb and phonograph. Held over 1,000 patents, his inventions greatly impacted modern electricity and sound recording.

  • Nikola Tesla (1856-1943)

    Pioneer of AC electricity and wireless transmission. His inventions and theoretical work laid the foundation for modern electrical engineering.

  • Alexander Graham Bell (1847-1922)

    Inventor of the telephone. His work focused on sound and speech, leading to significant advancements in communication technology.

  • Steve Jobs (1955-2011)

    Co-founder of Apple, revolutionized personal computing. His vision for user-friendly technology transformed multiple industries.

  • Frank Lloyd Wright (1867-1959)

    Revolutionary American architect and designer. Developed the Prairie School movement of architecture, influencing modern architectural design.

  • Imhotep (c. 2650-2600 BC)

    Ancient Egyptian architect, designed first pyramid. Considered the first architect in history, his innovations set standards for future architecture.
